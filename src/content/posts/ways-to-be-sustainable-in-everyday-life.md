## FAQ

<details>
  <summary>What is the most high-impact change I can make in my daily routine?</summary>
  <p>The most high-impact change is often related to **diet and consumption**. Significantly reducing or eliminating meat and dairy consumption (particularly beef) lowers your carbon footprint due to the resources required for livestock farming. Shopping locally and seasonally also helps.</p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>How can I reduce water consumption at home effectively?</summary>
  <p>Two easy, high-impact steps are **taking shorter showers** and being mindful of running water (e.g., turning off the tap while brushing your teeth). For laundry, ensure you always run the washing machine with a **full load** to maximize water efficiency per item.</p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What is the 'Refuse' principle, and how does it help with sustainability?</summary>
  <p>The 'Refuse' principle is the first and most critical of the 5 Rs (Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Rot). It means consciously **rejecting single-use items** and freebies you don't need, such as plastic straws, disposable cutlery, pamphlets, or unnecessary packaging, thereby stopping waste before it enters your home.</p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Beyond driving less, how can I make my transportation more sustainable?</summary>
  <p>If you must drive, ensure your vehicle is properly maintained (correct tire pressure, clean air filters) to maximize **fuel efficiency**. Better yet, switch to **public transport**, or choose walking and cycling for short errands, which also provides health benefits.</p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>How can I make my home energy consumption more sustainable without major renovations?</summary>
  <p>Switch to **LED light bulbs**, which use significantly less energy and last longer. Additionally, unplug electronics and appliances when not in use (**vampire power**), and use natural light and ventilation whenever possible to reduce reliance on heating/cooling systems.</p>
</details>
